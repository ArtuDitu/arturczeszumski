P R O F I L E
Artur Czeszumski is a Ph.D. Candidate in Cognitive Science at the University
of Osnabrück, and Clinical Psychology at the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam.
His research topics include social, computational, and cognitive neuroscience,
cognitive and clinical psychology, and embodied cognition. During his Ph.D., he
focused on studying cooperative behavior, and currently, he studies behavioral and
neural foundations of dyadic cooperative decision making.

was involved in all steps of the procedures. I helped to develop ideas and prepare
the grant proposal for two Niedersächsischen Innovationsförderprogramms
für Forschung und Entwicklung in Unternehmen (NBank) grants: EyeTrax

commission. Thus, I had experienced all aspects of acquiring grants.
O P E N S C I E N C E
I firmly believe that open science practices should be increasingly adapted in
cognitive neuroscience. That is why I actively participate in two international
projects that aim to address the reproducibility level of EEG research. In the
EEGManyLabs project, we replicate the most impactful EEG-ERP research. I lead the
replication of one study at the University of Osnabrück. In the EEGManyPipelines
project, we investigate the impact of different preprocessing and analysis pipelines
on EEG results. I lead preprocessing and analysis of one dataset at the University of
Osnabrück. Furthermore, I share online data and analysis code in projects where I
am a lead investigator. I will comply with open science principles to the best of my
abilities in my future work.
S K I L L S
Teaching
I have experience teaching and supervising many students. I have taught small
and large courses with a lot of success. Mainly, I am proud of my experience
of one-on-one supervision for BSc and MSc projects. I always try to elevate my
students while learning from their questions. On multiple occasions, my teaching
led to developing scientific manuscripts by my students (LightNET - networking
solution for performing dyadic VR experiments, comparison of the performance of
automated EEG preprocessing pipelines).
EEG
I have a great understanding of EEG data acquisition in stationary and mobile
settings. I am an expert in performing simple and advanced EEG analyses
(ERP, ERSP, time-frequency, TFCE, unFOLD, synchronization, hyperscanning).
Furthermore, I have offered help to all new students who would like to learn these
methods for many years.
Programming
I have an excellent command of R and MATLAB. I use the first one for statistical
analysis and computational modeling. I use the second to pre-process and analyze
EEG data. I believe that programming is a crucial skill for cognitive science students,
which I am constantly trying to improve and offer my help to students making their
first steps with coding.
Science communication
I genuinely love meeting and discussing science with other scientists. That is why
I engaged with an above-average conference attendance rate during my Ph.D.
Giving many talks and poster presentations allowed me to develop excellent skills
in science communication.
